Mapo Tofu

Description

Mapo Tofu is a spicy Chinese dish that focuses on soft tofu cooked in a rich, spicy, and savory sauce. It is typically served over rice and is enjoyed by all

ingredients

  • 1 pound of firm tofu
  • 1/2 pound ground pork or beef
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (fermented bean paste)
  • 2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced/li>
  • 2-3 dried red chili pepper, chopped
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons corn starch dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1/4 cup of chicken or beef broth/li>
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  1. Drain the tofu and cut it into small cubes. Set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat the oil and brown the ground meat.
  3. Add the doubanjiang, Sichuan peppercorns, ginger, garlic, and chili pepper. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the broth, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add the tofu and gently stir to coat with the sauce.
  6. Add the cornstarch mixture and stir until the sauce thickens.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot with rice.
